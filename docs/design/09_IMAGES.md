# 09 Images

## Image Philosophy

Images must feel curated, consistent, and materially authentic. The photography system should make products appear premium, tactile, and trustworthy rather than overproduced.

## Image Ratio

- Use a small set of approved ratios and apply them consistently.
- Hero imagery may use wider cinematic framing.
- Product imagery should maintain stable framing across cards and detail views.
- Gallery imagery may vary, but only within a controlled visual family.

## Compression

- Compress aggressively enough to protect performance without visible degradation.
- Preserve detail in material surfaces, stitching, finishes, and edges.
- Avoid over-sharpened or artifact-heavy output.

## Lazy Loading

- Lazy load all non-critical imagery below the fold.
- Hero media may load eagerly if it is essential to the first impression.
- Do not delay visible content in favor of image perfection.

## Image Style

- Natural, composed, and quiet.
- Consistent cropping and alignment across collections.
- Product images should feel like a premium catalogue, not marketplace listings.

## Lighting

- Use soft, directional light with realistic shadow control.
- Prefer warm-neutral environments over harsh contrast.
- Avoid overexposed highlights and cheap studio gloss.

## Editing Style

- Color correction should remain natural.
- Remove visual noise while preserving authenticity.
- Keep background treatment consistent across categories.
- Avoid dramatic filters and trendy post-processing.

## Consistency

- All product images must feel like they belong to one brand system.
- Backgrounds, angles, and tonal treatment should be standardized.
- Maintain visual continuity across homepage, category pages, and project galleries.

## Image Rules

- Never use low-resolution or inconsistent aspect ratios in the same grid.
- Never mix overly stylized imagery with plain catalogue shots.
- Never compromise load speed for decorative image complexity.
- Images should support trust first and beauty second.
