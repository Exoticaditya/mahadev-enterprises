# 10 Video Guidelines

## Video Philosophy

Video should add atmosphere and credibility only when still imagery cannot communicate the experience fully. It must remain light, elegant, and performance-safe.

## Hero Video

- Use only if it strengthens the first impression.
- Prefer short, visually calm loops or ambient sequences.
- It should support the editorial hero, not overpower it.

## Background Video

- Background video is optional and should be used sparingly.
- It must not reduce readability of any foreground content.
- Keep motion slow and non-distracting.

## Gallery Video

- Use for material movement, studio context, or product demonstration when needed.
- Keep clips short and purposeful.
- Video should complement the product narrative.

## Loop Rules

- Loops must be seamless or nearly seamless.
- Avoid visible jump cuts.
- Keep loop lengths short enough to remain subtle.

## Compression

- Use modern compression to preserve quality while protecting performance.
- Keep file sizes small enough for fast mobile delivery.
- Do not ship heavy assets for decorative purposes alone.

## Autoplay

- Autoplay only when muted and clearly safe for user experience.
- Never force sound.
- Provide visible controls if the video contains meaningful narrative content.

## Performance

- Video must never jeopardize Core Web Vitals.
- Use poster images and fallbacks.
- Avoid video on pages where still images communicate just as well.
- Keep the experience excellent on mobile data connections.

## Video Rules

- One compelling short clip is better than many noisy assets.
- Video should feel calm, premium, and editorial.
- Never use video as a substitute for weak photography or weak copy.
