# 07 Layouts

## Layout Philosophy

Layouts should feel architectural, spacious, and editorial. Every breakpoint must preserve clarity, hierarchy, and calm density.

## Desktop

- Primary experience for editorial storytelling and product discovery.
- Use generous grid spacing and wide composition.
- Support side-by-side content and media where the layout benefits.

## Tablet

- Keep hierarchy intact while reducing breadth.
- Stack elements more aggressively when readability benefits.
- Avoid layouts that feel like compressed desktop pages.

## Mobile

- Mobile is a first-class layout, not a fallback.
- Reduce complexity and preserve touch comfort.
- Keep CTAs visible but tasteful.
- Avoid cramped multi-column structures.

## Ultra Wide

- Maintain a strong maximum readable width for text.
- Allow wider media moments where appropriate.
- Prevent content from stretching into weakly composed lines.

## 4K

- Use measured scaling, not sheer expansion.
- Preserve proportion and typographic control.
- Limit the width of text-heavy sections to maintain editorial quality.

## 8K

- The layout should scale with restraint and maintain compositional discipline.
- Space may increase, but information density must remain deliberate.
- Never allow blankness to become emptiness.

## Breakpoints

Breakpoints should be defined around content needs rather than device names alone.

- Small mobile
- Large mobile
- Tablet
- Small desktop
- Desktop
- Large desktop
- Ultra wide

## Layout Rules

- Never depend on a single breakpoint for major content shifts.
- Preserve the same story across all screen sizes.
- Reflow content, do not reimagine the experience per device.
- Keep the site visually premium at every width.
