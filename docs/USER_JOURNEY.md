# User Journey

## Stage 1: Discovery

Users arrive through search, referrals, social links, or direct brand recall. They immediately evaluate visual quality, category relevance, and whether the company looks credible enough to continue.

## Stage 2: Orientation

Visitors scan the homepage, product categories, and trust signals to understand what is sold, who it is for, and whether the brand suits commercial or premium use cases.

## Stage 3: Evaluation

Users compare product groups, read about capability, check project suitability, and look for signs of reliability such as export experience, material quality, and responsive enquiry handling.

## Stage 4: Action

The user submits an enquiry, requests a catalogue, or opens a direct contact path. This is where the website must reduce effort and create confidence.

## Stage 5: Follow-up

After the first visit, users may return through organic search or remarketing. The site should support repeated consideration with consistent content, clear metadata, and memorable design.

## Key Journey Principles

- Minimize friction at every stage.
- Make trust visible early.
- Let users self-identify by use case.
- Keep enquiry CTAs persistent but tasteful.
- Avoid overwhelming visitors with product clutter.

## Journey Success Criteria

A user should be able to understand the business, locate the right category, confirm suitability, and contact the team in under a minute from any high-intent page.
