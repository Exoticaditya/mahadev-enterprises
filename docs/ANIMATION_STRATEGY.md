# Animation Strategy

## Motion Philosophy

Animation should feel like quiet confidence. It must support perception of quality without distracting from the content or affecting performance.

## Core Motion Rules

- Use motion sparingly and intentionally.
- Prefer subtle transitions over dramatic effects.
- Motion should clarify hierarchy, not decorate it.
- Avoid excessive parallax or gimmicky scroll effects.

## Recommended Motion Patterns

- Gentle hero entrance with staggered text reveal.
- Section fade and rise on scroll.
- Hover transitions for cards and links.
- Smooth mobile menu and modal transitions.
- Lightweight state feedback for forms and CTA actions.

## Interaction Goals

- Improve perceived polish.
- Help users understand structure and hierarchy.
- Reinforce premium craftsmanship.
- Maintain excellent performance on mobile devices.

## Performance Guardrails

- Respect reduced-motion preferences.
- Avoid large animation trees on initial load.
- Limit the number of simultaneous animated elements.
- Keep animation timing elegant and consistent.
