# Target Audience

## Primary Segments

### Yoga Studios and Yoga Schools
- Need durable props, mats, accessories, and teaching support items.
- Care about consistency, pricing bands, and repeat ordering.
- Prefer suppliers that understand training environments.

### Pilates Studios
- Need premium equipment presentation and performance confidence.
- Value aesthetics, longevity, and commercial suitability.
- Often require a more refined brand experience.

### Hotels and Resorts
- Need products that fit guest-facing wellness experiences.
- Care about elegance, safety, and easy procurement.
- Look for dependable delivery and professional communication.

### Retreat Centres and Wellness Brands
- Need curated, calming, design-aligned products.
- Focus on experience quality and brand resonance.
- Often purchase in batches for launches or seasonal refreshes.

### Interior Designers and Commercial Specifiers
- Need products that integrate with spatial and visual concepts.
- Require fast access to specifications, finish options, and styling context.
- Want a supplier who feels organized and design-literate.

### International Buyers
- Need export confidence, clarity, and responsiveness.
- Often compare across websites before engaging.
- Need strong trust signals and concise product discovery.

## Buyer Priorities

- Quality and durability.
- Visual sophistication.
- Commercial pricing logic.
- Lead time and fulfilment confidence.
- Ability to support custom or bulk requirements.

## Key Emotional Drivers

- Confidence that the supplier is serious.
- Relief from uncertainty around quality and delivery.
- Pride in choosing a beautiful, premium product source.
- Assurance that the brand understands wellness culture.

## Website Implication

The site should speak simultaneously to rational procurement needs and emotional brand expectations. This means concise proof, calm presentation, and a purchase path that feels professional rather than transactional.
