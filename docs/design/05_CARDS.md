# 05 Cards

## Card Philosophy

Cards must feel like well-composed editorial objects, not default UI containers. Their job is to organize information elegantly while preserving the calm premium tone of the brand.

## Product Cards

- Should foreground product name, category, and one primary value cue.
- Include imagery with disciplined cropping and consistent framing.
- Keep supporting details minimal and scan-friendly.
- Encourage the user to open detail or enquire, not to over-read the card.

## Blog Cards

- Use editorial hierarchy with strong title, short summary, and metadata.
- Avoid excessive badges or noisy category markers.
- Should feel like magazine features, not content marketing tiles.

## Gallery Cards

- Prioritize imagery with minimal text overlay.
- Use for projects, styling inspiration, and material detail.
- May include one subtle label or caption only.

## Quote Cards

- Should feel calm, credible, and restrained.
- Use large quotation marks sparingly or not at all if they add noise.
- Keep attribution clear and secondary.

## Glass Cards

- Allowed only in select hero or floating contexts.
- Must maintain readability in both themes.
- Should feel like a premium layer above the content, not an effect for its own sake.

## Hover Animations

- Slight lift, border refinement, or image scale only.
- The hover state should suggest quality and responsiveness.
- Never use aggressive motion or large shadow jumps.
- Hover must be slower and softer than the click action.

## Card Rules

- Keep card heights aligned within a grid where possible.
- Use consistent internal spacing and hierarchy.
- Do not stack too many actions inside a single card.
- Cards should guide the eye without overwhelming it.
