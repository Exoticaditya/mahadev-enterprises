# 02 Typography

## Typographic Direction

Typography must carry the premium character of the site. The system should feel editorial, intelligent, and calm, with clear hierarchy and generous rhythm.

## Heading Hierarchy

- **Display / H1:** Homepage hero and major campaign intros.
- **H2:** Major section headings and page-level statements.
- **H3:** Product group labels, use-case sections, and subtopics.
- **H4:** Feature labels, trust points, and card titles.
- **Body Large:** Lead paragraphs and editorial introductions.
- **Body:** Main page copy.
- **Body Small:** Supporting details, labels, and metadata.
- **Caption:** Spec notes, tags, and auxiliary content.

## Font Families

### Primary Display Family

- Preferred: **Canela**
- Alternative: **Cormorant Garamond**
- Purpose: Hero statements, large editorial lines, and luxury brand moments.

### Primary UI Family

- Preferred: **Inter** or **Neue Haas Grotesk**
- Alternative: **Source Sans 3**
- Purpose: Navigation, forms, product data, and all practical interface text.

### Supporting Monospace Family

- Preferred: **IBM Plex Mono**
- Purpose: Technical specs, part numbers, dimensions, and internal-style reference data.

## Font Pairing

- **Display + UI:** Canela + Inter
- **Display + Mono:** Canela + IBM Plex Mono for data-led sections
- **Fallback Strategy:** If premium licensed fonts are unavailable, preserve the same contrast using a refined serif display face and a neutral grotesk UI family.

The pairing must always communicate that the brand is both design-conscious and operationally serious.

## Responsive Typography

Typography must scale smoothly across devices without abrupt jumps.

- Use a large hero scale on desktop with controlled line length.
- Reduce display size on tablet while keeping strong presence.
- Use shorter headline lines on mobile, avoiding cramped multi-line compositions.
- Body copy should remain comfortable for reading at arm’s length.

## Letter Spacing

- **Display Large:** `-0.04em` to `-0.02em`
- **H1:** `-0.03em` to `-0.015em`
- **H2 / H3:** `-0.02em` to `0em`
- **Uppercase Labels:** `0.08em` to `0.14em`
- **Buttons:** `0.02em` to `0.05em`

Letter spacing should feel tailored, not stylized.

## Line Height

- **Display:** `0.95` to `1.05`
- **Headings:** `1.05` to `1.2`
- **Body Large:** `1.5` to `1.7`
- **Body:** `1.6` to `1.8`
- **Small Copy:** `1.4` to `1.6`

## Body Sizes

- **Desktop Body:** 17 to 18 px equivalent
- **Tablet Body:** 16 to 17 px equivalent
- **Mobile Body:** 15 to 16 px equivalent
- **Supporting Notes:** 13 to 14 px equivalent

## Button Typography

- Use medium weight or semibold weight.
- Keep labels concise and action-led.
- Maintain slight letter spacing for refinement.
- Avoid all-caps unless a label is intentionally editorial.

## Display Typography

- Hero headlines should be large, calm, and image-compatible.
- Avoid overly dramatic words broken across too many lines.
- Prefer short, confident statements with strong vertical rhythm.
- Display copy should feel like a magazine cover, not a sales banner.

## Typographic Rules

- Never use more than two core font families in the UI.
- Avoid novelty fonts and decorative scripts.
- Maintain predictable hierarchy on every page type.
- Use typographic weight, not color noise, to create emphasis.
